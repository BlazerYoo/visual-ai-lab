Mission statement

The Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory’s Outreach Summer program is intended to increase diversity in the field of Artificial Intelligence. Our mission is to inspire tomorrow’s leaders to know, think and use Artificial Intelligence. We aim to educate students through a combination of lectures, hands-on research projects, field trips, and mentoring activities. We are committed to serving students of all financial backgrounds.

SAILORS curriculum

SAILORS is short for Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory's OutReach Summer program. Through its carefully designed two-week curriculum, SAILORS aims to addresses three key problems of STEM recruitment: 1) lack of exposure to technical concepts early on; 2) limited perception of computer science as a field; and 3) lack of same-sex mentors and peer support. Studies have shown that women and minorities are particularly drawn to and motivated by humanitarian applications of computing, and are often alienated by the existing homogeneous STEM culture. Our program teaches technically rigorous AI concepts in the context of societal impact. Students choose from research projects that each applies AI technical skills towards a humanitarian purpose: 1) making hospitals safer by utilizing computer vision, 2) assisting disaster relief through natural language processing of tweets from affected areas, 3) identifying cancer cells using biomedical informatics, and 4) making driving safer by studying autonomous vehicle technology.

SAILORS history

In December 2014, I walked into Fei-Fei Li's office and said, Do you want to run an AI camp for high school girls together? and she said, YESSS! So we started the annual SAILORS program to bring a small group of high school students to the Stanford AI lab for two weeks over the summer and teach them about AI. The program is targeting 10th grade girls. We've been receiving more than 200 student applications for 24 slots -- clearly, SAILORS is a much-needed endeavor! Many AI professors, graduate students and postdocs contribute their time and expertise to helping make SAILORS happen. We're incredibly lucky to have my long-time friend and THE expert on running academic camps Rick Sommer (who directs Stanford's Pre-Collegiate Summer Institutes) co-directing SAILORS with us. Fei-Fei, Rick and I, along with the Stanford AI Lab and the Pre-Collegiate Studies team, are committed to running SAILORS for many years to come.

Future plans

  • The SAILORS alumni girls are working on several year-around outreach initiatives, speading the lessons of SAILORS to local communities
  • We're working on turning SAILORS into a residential program by the summer of 2017
  • We're planning a sister program for SAILORS housed at Princeton University, aiming for summer of 2018
