PRINCETON AI4ALL
Princeton AI4ALL is a three-week summer camp for rising high school juniors co-founded in 2018 by Professor Olga Russakovsky (who also co-founded Stanford AI4ALL in 2015) and Professor Ed Felten. Each year, we work with a new cohort of approximately 30 students, guiding them through hands-on research projects, professor lectures, and a field trip to Washington, D.C. to discuss AI policy and ethics.

AI4ALL nonprofit
AI4ALL is a national nonprofit co-founded by Professor Olga Russakovsky. It is dedicated to creating a more inclusive, human-centered discipline by transforming the pipeline of AI practitioners.
PRINCETON Lab Learning Program
We collaborate with the Princeton Lab Learning Program (LLP) to provide high school students with opportunities to do research in our lab some summers.

PRINCETON AI REU
The computer science department at Princeton invites students to apply to participate in a research experience for undergraduates (REU) focused on AI. We are excited to be a part of this effort.
PRINCETON AI Lab
The Princeton Laboratory for Artificial Intelligence (the "AI Lab" for short) supports and expands the scope of AI research at Princeton. The AI Lab is focused on incubating ambitious AI-related research initiatives involving faculty members across the natural sciences, engineering, social sciences and humanities and providing a shared infrastructure for supporting AI research more broadly. Professor Olga Russakovsky is excited to be the Associate Director of this effort.
